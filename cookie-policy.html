1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit it. They allow the website to remember your actions and preferences over time, improving your experience. Cookies can be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (remaining on your device for a set period).

2. How We Use Cookies

Apollume uses cookies for the following purposes:

Category Purpose Examples
Essential Required for core website functionality. Cannot be disabled. Session management, security tokens
Analytics Help us understand how visitors interact with our site (page views, traffic sources, bounce rates). Aggregated usage statistics
Performance Improve website speed and reliability. CDN preferences, load balancing
Preference Remember your settings and choices. Language preferences, UI settings

We do not use cookies for advertising or behavioural tracking, and we do not share cookie data with advertising networks.

  • Cloudflare (cdnjs) — for delivering the Three.js library used in our visual effects. See Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.

We do not control the cookies placed by these third parties and encourage you to review their policies directly.

4. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies.
  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies.
  • Receive a notification when a cookie is set.

Please note that disabling essential cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5. Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. As there is no universally agreed standard for responding to DNT signals, our website does not currently alter its behaviour in response to them. We will revisit this position as standards evolve.
